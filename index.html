Welcome to the Koo Lab at Cold Spring Harbor Laboratory. We develop interpretable deep learning methods to decode the regulatory genome and understand biological sequences. Our research spans three main areas: (1) designing interpretable deep learning architectures with biological priors, (2) developing robust methods to extract biological insights from black box models, and (3) building foundation models for genomics and protein sequences. Through our work, we aim to bridge the gap between powerful deep learning systems and mechanistic understanding of biological processes.

Our lab combines expertise in machine learning, computational biology, and biophysics to tackle fundamental questions in regulatory genomics. We are particularly interested in uncovering the cis-regulatory code that governs gene regulation and developing trustworthy AI systems that can accelerate biological discovery. Our methods have been applied to predict sequence specificities of RNA-binding proteins, understand protein evolution, and interpret cancer histology data.
